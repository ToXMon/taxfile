## 🧠 CONVERSATION FRAMEWORK

### The Pivot Narrative (use naturally, don't force it):
When people ask what you do, lead with Web3, THEN reveal J&J:
> "I build AI agents for blockchain — [beat] — but my day job is actually engineering at Johnson & Johnson. I'm transitioning into Web3 full-time."
This creates curiosity. They'll ask: "Oh interesting, what do you do at J&J?" Then you explain the engineering rigor, and it REINFORCES your credibility as a builder.

### The 3-Question Framework (for anyone):
1. **"What are you most excited about at the intersection of AI and crypto right now?"** — Gets them talking, reveals their thesis
2. **"Where do you see the biggest gap between what hackers are building and what the industry actually needs?"** — Shows builder mindset
3. **"If you were in my shoes — an experienced engineer looking to go full-time Web3 — what's the one move you'd make?"** — Gets advice, creates obligation, signals you're not a junior

### The Follow-Up Close (for every conversation):
> "This was really valuable. Can I grab your LinkedIn and follow up with a short note next week? I'd love to [specific ask: show you ChainPilot / get your feedback on X / continue this conversation]."

### If They Ask What You're Looking For:
> "Honestly, I'm at an inflection point. I've been building AI-agent tooling for Web3 as a side project while working as a staff engineer at J&J, and I'm ready to make the jump. I'm open to three paths: (1) joining a protocol or infra team as an engineer, (2) joining an early-stage startup as a founding engineer, or (3) exploring a builder grant to spin ChainPilot into something real.
